## 摘要
警觉性的波动塑造了感知和行为，然而它们如何影响大脑跨感觉整合信息的能力仍知之甚少。在这里，我们研究了当人类从清醒过渡到睡眠时，多感觉整合是否得以保留。参与者在进行听觉-触觉检测任务时，脑电图追踪了自发警觉性下降。行为上，尽管反应变慢且遗漏增加，多感觉刺激在嗜睡期间仍继续促进反应。尽管竞赛模型预测与多感觉整合保留的证据一致，但多感觉相互作用的事件相关特征在嗜睡期间减弱或不再可检测。关键的是，多变量解码显示，多感觉神经表征仍然可检测，尽管时间稳定性降低，并且早期跨状态泛化揭示了跨警觉性水平的共享表征结构，表明核心多感觉计算得以保留。这些发现表明，多感觉整合在警觉性下降时仍然功能正常但动态改变，揭示了大脑在从清醒到睡眠过渡期间如何维持适应性行为。

意义声明：多感觉整合常被描述为一种稳定且自动的感知过程。然而，越来越多的证据表明，它可以被当前的大脑状态和认知需求所调节。在这里，我们探讨了当警觉性下降时，这一计算是否保持不变。我们表明，在嗜睡期间，多感觉整合既没有被废除，也不是完全不变的。相反，尽管神经动态不那么稳定，行为益处仍然保持。尽管事件相关反应减弱，但核心多感觉表征在警觉性水平上仍然共享。这些发现表明，多感觉整合在警觉性波动中得以保留，而它们的神经动态随着警觉性下降而灵活改变。

## Abstract
Fluctuations in alertness shape perception and behaviour, yet how they affect the brains ability to integrate information across senses remains poorly understood. Here we investigated whether multisensory integration is preserved as humans transition from wakefulness to sleep. Participants (18 females, 8 males) performed an audio-tactile detection task while electroencephalography tracked spontaneous declines in alertness. Behaviourally, multisensory stimulation continued to facilitate responses during drowsiness, despite slowing and increased omissions. Although race-model predictions were consistent with preserved evidence for multisensory integration, event-related signatures of multisensory interactions were attenuated or no longer detectable during drowsiness. Crucially, multivariate decoding showed that multisensory neural representations remained detectable, although less temporally stable, and early cross-state generalization revealed shared representational structure across alertness levels, indicating preserved core multisensory computations. These findings show that multisensory integration remains functional but dynamically altered as alertness declines, revealing how the brain maintains adaptive behaviour during the wake-to-sleep transition.

Significance StatementMultisensory integration has often been described as a stable and automatic perceptual process. However, growing evidence indicates that it can be modulated by ongoing brain states and cognitive demands. Here, we ask whether this computation remains invariant as alertness declines. We show that multisensory integration is neither abolished nor fully invariant during drowsiness. Instead, behavioural benefits are maintained despite less stable neural dynamics. Although event-related responses are attenuated, core multisensory representations remain shared across levels of alertness. These findings indicate that multisensory integration is preserved across fluctuations in alertness, while their neural dynamics are flexibly altered as alertness decreases.
